    Energy of the compressed air

                                  

    Using Boyle law we get that

    E = 10(xLx-x+1)    where

    E = Energy in Kilogrameters in a tank of one liter volume.

    One kilogrameter is a force of one kilo in one meter, is the same energy of 9.81 Joules.

    367000 Kilogrameters is a KWH

    KWH = Kilowatt hour

    Kgm = Kilogrameter

    The absolute pressure in the tank is x Kg/cm the absolute pressure is the relative pressure plus one.

    Example: The pressure in the gauge of the tank is 10 kg /cm absolute pressure of the tank is 10+1=11 kg/cm

    E = 10(11L11-11+1) = 163.758 Kgm

    L11 = 2.3978

    2.3978x11 =26.3758

    26.3578-11+1 = 16.3758

    16.3578x10 = 163.758

    A tank with compressed air at 200 Kg/cm of 50 liters volume have 8659x50= 432982 Kgm of energy or 432982/367000 = 1.17 KWH

  • Report 36 (6 /4 / 2002)

    29,4,4,1,36

    By Armando Regusci

    Description of the Regusci engine and why this engine is the best option.

    The Regusci engine consist in a free piston and a free wheel, the connections between the free piston and the free wheel can be done by different ways.

    The engine can have one or several piston and these pistons can be equal or different for the same engine.

    The piston can be connected to one or several free wheel and the free wheel can be equal or different.

    The piston can be simple or double effect and with different diameter and stroke.

    An example of this engines is a cylinder very long in relation with his diameter, a piston connected to a shaft, when we put air with pressure inside the cylinder the air push the piston the piston pull the shaft, the shaft pull a chain the chain turn the free wheel and the free wheel turn the shaft of the engine. The engine have two valves, one for let the air go inside the cylinder from the tank or other cylinder and the other for let the air go from the cylinder to the atmosphere or other cylinder.

    The engine has a spring for return the cylinder to the initial position.

    The advantages of this engine are:

    1) The cylinder can be much longer in relation with his diameter; this advantage let open the valve for put the air in and the valve for put the air out only one time instead of several times.

    This advantage is very important when the engine works at very high pressures for example 700 Kg /cm or 9940 PSI.

    If we use a piston with one cm square of surface the force is 700 kilos and we can use this force in the engine without problem, in the engine the cylinder can by 40 cm stroke, in a piston connected to a crankshaft the valve open and shut 20 times instead of one and we lose energy, if we use a piston with 10 cm of diameter it open 4 times but the surface is the force is 54950 kilos and the engine will be broken.

    This let us open the where the air came to the cylinder for 20 cm and afterward shut it and decompress the air at half of his pressure.

    2) This engine can run at very high pressure for example 700 kilos or 9940 PSI, if we decompress at 35.21 Kilos we only get 48 % of the energy.

    3) If we don’t need it we don’t use it

    For example in an engine with 10 pistons we only use one if we don’t need to much power, but if we need all the power we can use all the pistons.

    If we don’t need power, we don’t use the engine and it run in freewheel.

    When we use all the power, we need to let go to the atmosphere a lot of air, in an engine with crankshaft, we keep moving this lot of air even if we don’t need power.

    In a Regusci engine if we don’t need it we don’t use it.

    On the top of the stroke the efficiency of the crankshaft is cero.

    In the Regusci engine, on the top of the stroke we have the same excellent efficiency that we have in all the stroke.

    4 The piston is stop and with out volume when we put air inside the cylinder

    This give more efficiency, in an engine with crankshaft this is impossible.

    5 When the piston is at the maximum of his pressure when start the movement the mechanical efficiency is the same that for all the positions of the piston and is very high, in an engine with crankshaft the efficiency is very bad when the piston start the movement.

    6 We can use a spring for reduce the force when the piston start the movement and afterwards get this energy, the spring is located between the piston and the free wheel.

    7 When the pressure of the tank is low we can only use the pistons with high diameter.

    8 The engine has a big volumetric efficiency and because there is not force between the piston and the cylinder, it have a long life efficiency, that’s doesn’t happen in and engine with crankshaft.

    9 The engine has very good mechanical efficiency for all the positions of the piston.

    10 We can put exactly the air that we want each time that the engine works. We can let the air go to the atmosphere at the atmosphere pressure and get all the energy of expansion.

    If we need more power we can let the air go to the atmosphere at more pressure than the atmosphere pressure and get more power, but with less efficiency.

    11 The efficiency of the Carnot cycle is much better.

    29,4,4,1,37

    By Armando Regusci

    Some important things that Armando Regusci did in vehicles that run with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel, compressed air and liquid air until 3/12/2002.

    In 1959 when he was 19 years old he got the idea of vehicles that run with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel, compressed air and liquid air.

    In 1974 he got 2 patents of the vehicles with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel.

    In 1975 he got the first press publication of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel.

    In 1977 he got 2 patents of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel; he got the first prototype running.

    In 1978 he got one press publication of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel.

    In 1979 he got 12 press publications of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel in Uruguay, Argentina, Mexico and Italy, and also some interviews on TV.

    In 1980 he got 13 press publications of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel in several countries and several interview on TV.

    In 1981 he got one patent of the vehicle with hydrostatic transmission and flywheel and got 2 press publications.

    In 1984 he started to work in compressed air vehicles.

    In 1986 he got the first prototype that run with compressed air, he got the first patent of vehicles with liquid air, the first patent of the vehicles with compressed air and got the first press publication of vehicles of compressed air and liquid air.

    In 1987 he got the first patent of the engine with free piston and free wheel that he call Regusci engine and got one press publication of vehicles with compressed air.

    In 1988 he got one press publication of vehicles with compressed air and got the first car with compressed air running.

    In 1989 he got one patent of vehicles with compressed air.

    In 1990 he got the second patent of the engine that run with free piston and free wheel that he call Regusci engine.

    In 1991 he got 2 press publications of the vehicle with compressed air.

    In 1992 he got 3 patents one of the Regusci engine.

    One about turbines for compressed air vehicles and an other one about a system to get compressed air to a vehicle in just one minute, he got one press publication of vehicles with compressed air, run 100 Kilometers with only one charge of compressed air and got the Genesis Prize (Prize to Best Scientific Research) for one of his patents of vehicles with compressed air.

    In 1993 he went to USA for develop the compressed air vehicles there.

    He wrote to Vehicle Research Institute of Washington University, to FORD, to GM and other companies, and he didn’t get any help.

    He got one press publication and one interview on TV in MIAMI.

    In 1994 he wrote to DOE Department of Energy and he didn’t get any help.

    In 1995 he built 2 prototypes in USA with compressed air.

    In 1996 the TV in New Jersey made a video of one of his vehicles running with compressed air and asked for the videos of the car running in Uruguay with compressed air, but those two videos never been air.

    This year, also show one of the vehicles running with compressed air in several exhibition centers in the US. One of them, at the Hobby Center (New Jersey) where thousands of people can see it running and in several cities of USA.

    In 1997 he wrote to The New York Times, Scientific American, Popular Science, Washington Post and Popular Mechanic and only the New York Times told me that can’t help me.

    He returns to Uruguay, he got one press publication of vehicles with compressed air.

    In 1998 he built 2 prototypes and got 5 press publications of vehicles with compressed air.

    In 1999 he got 10 press publications of vehicles with compressed air and showed the vehicle running in several cities in Uruguay.

    He got several interview on the TV.

    In 2000 he got 2 press publications of vehicles with compressed air show the vehicle running in several cities in Uruguay and he got several interviews on TV.

    In 2001 he received a call from UNT (University of North Texas), they asked Professor Regusci to come over and develop a compress air engine.

    He worked with liquid nitrogen vehicles, and with the same among of liquid nitrogen run 300km, instead of the 30km that the UNT prototype was running.

    Got 2 patents.

    In 2002 he built a motorcycle with compressed air, and in the first test run 20 miles with only one charge of compressed air (march 12).

    In 2003 he came back to Uruguay, satisfied for the achievement on the UNT research, but very sad because again he didn´t get any help.

    That year Professor Regusci, understood that was fighting against the system, and he start to plan a new strategy to succeed

    He never gave up, and today he is still working.
